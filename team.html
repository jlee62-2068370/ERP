Willis Tang

Co-Founder & Chief Executive Officer

Willis is currently a third-year undergraduate student at the University of Southern California. He is a licensed EMT-B and has extensive clinical experience from volunteering as an EMT and working as a Wound Care Technician throughout his academic career. He is also a two-time recipient of the USC Provost Research Fellowship and an Undergraduate Fellowship in Addiction Science (UFAS) awardee, conducting research on robot-assisted motor learning in infants and the effects of tobacco use on pulmonary health. He also has a passion for education, working with students all throughout K-12 as both a volunteer tutor and college guidance counselor. With ERP, Willis hopes to promote bystander confidence in providing medical care and bolster levels of medical competency within the communities across Los Angeles.

Inaya Riaz

Co-Founder & Chief Operations Officer

Inaya is a candidate for a M.S. in Global Medicine at the University of Southern California’s Keck School of Medicine. She completed her undergraduate studies at USC with a B.A. in Medical Anthropology and a minor in Dance, where her research focused on the biokinetics of dance medicine. Inaya has worked as an EMT since 2018, holding various positions in ambulance services, collegiate stand-by services, and disaster relief. Andrew Kinoshita

Director of Governance

Andrew is currently a student at the University of Southern California pursuing a Master of Public Health. He leads the Health Sciences Education Program, an organization that educates K-12 students in South Los Angeles on relevant health topics to encourage concrete steps towards the maintenance of healthy living. Andrew also currently interns with the California Department of Public Health to address substance use and addiction prevention, and he is a licensed EMT-B volunteering with the Emergency Medical Services of USC. With the Everyday Responder Project, Andrew is looking to help community members learn the skills necessary to respond while emergency services are still en route.
